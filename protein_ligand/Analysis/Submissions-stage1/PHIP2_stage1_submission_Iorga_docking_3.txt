Method:
From literature data we have created a validation dataset containing 1499 compounds, with 45 active compounds among them (identified by X-ray crystallography and/or AlphaScreen assay). This represents 3% of active compounds identified overall.
We have also identified in the PDB 8 crystal structures containing fragment-like ligands.
From the analysis of conformations of the four binding sites indicated by the organizers in these structures we found 3 representative conformations for site 1, 2 conformations for site 2, 1 conformation for site 3 and 3 conformations for site 4.
3D conformations of compounds from validation and prediction datasets were generated from SMILES using CORINA  v4.2.0, then protonated to physiological pH with Schrodinger LigPrep v48012.
The 1499 compounds were docked on each conformation of the four binding sites using Gold with 4 scoring functions: GoldScore, ChemScore, ASP and ChemPLP. 50 poses were generated for each ligand in all docking calculations.
For timing reasons only the docking poses for site 1 were analyzed, by looking at the ranking of the 45 active compounds and at the pose prediction (RMSD values calculated using in house scripts and CACTVS Chemoinformatics Toolkit V3.4.6.26) for the ligands from the 8 crystal structures. In both cases, ChemScore with the second conformation of site 1 provided significantly better results (although not very satisfactory) compared to the other combinations of scoring functions and site conformations.
Then, the 799 compounds provided by the organizers in the prediction dataset were docked in the second conformation of site 1, using the same protocol and docking parameters as above. As the number of active compounds in this dataset is not known and the validation calculations couldn't provide a clear cutoff scoring value to distinguish actives from inactives, we decided to submit 3 predictions, with top 5%, 10% and 3% of ranked compounds predicted as active.
This submission should not be ranked and corresponds to the top 3% (24 compounds) predicted as active, binding to site 1.
